The Psychology of Objections

Before diving into scripts, understand what's really happening when prospects object:

Fear-Based Objections

95% of objections stem from fear of making the wrong decision. Your job is to reduce perceived risk, not increase pressure.

Control Objections

Prospects want to feel in control of the process. Frame responses to give them choices, not ultimatums.

Information Objections

Sometimes "no" means "I don't understand." Clarify benefits and address specific concerns with facts.

The Feel-Felt-Found Formula

This three-step pattern acknowledges their concern, shows they're not alone, then provides proof:

"I understand how you feel... many of my clients felt the same way... here's what they found when they moved forward..."

Advanced Techniques for Persistent Objections

The Takeaway Technique

When facing strong resistance, sometimes the best approach is to remove pressure:

"You know what? I'm sensing this isn't the right fit, and that's perfectly okay. Let me give you the names of two other excellent agents who might be better suited for your situation."

This often triggers a reversal because people want what they think they can't have.

The Future Pace Close

Help prospects visualize the positive outcome:

"Imagine it's three months from now and your home just sold for $25,000 more than you expected. How would that change your plans for your next home?"

The Alternative Choice Close

Instead of yes/no decisions, offer two positive alternatives:

"Would you prefer to start with a market analysis this week, or would next week work better for you?"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't Do This:

  • Get defensive or argumentative
  • Use high-pressure tactics
  • Ignore the emotional component
  • Rush through your response
  • Use the same script for every person
  • Fail to confirm understanding

Do This Instead:

  • Acknowledge their concern first
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Share relevant stories/examples
  • Pause and let them respond
  • Adapt your approach to their style
  • Confirm agreement before moving forward
