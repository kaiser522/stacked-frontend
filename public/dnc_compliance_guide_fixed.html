The Do Not Call (DNC) registry and related regulations protect consumers from unwanted marketing calls and texts. For real estate professionals using calling and texting platforms, compliance isn't optional‚Äîit's critical to avoid massive penalties and protect your business reputation.

This guide covers federal and state requirements, platform-specific compliance features, and actionable steps to ensure your outreach stays within legal boundaries.

Key Regulations Overview

Multiple federal laws govern calling and texting for business purposes:

TCPA (1991)

Telephone Consumer Protection Act - Regulates autodialed calls, prerecorded messages, and text messages to cell phones

TSR (1995)

Telemarketing Sales Rule - Governs telemarketing practices and maintains the National DNC Registry

CAN-SPAM (2003)

Controls commercial email and extends to SMS marketing with consent and opt-out requirements

Penalty Structure

Up to $46,517

Per violation for TCPA infractions. Multiply this by hundreds of calls and you could face millions in penalties.

Established Business Relationship (EBR) Exception

You can call people on the DNC registry if you have an established business relationship within the last 18 months, but you must honor any request to stop calling immediately.

Dialer Compliance Requirements

Automatic dialing systems have strict legal requirements under the TCPA:

TCPA Dialer Rules

1
Prior Express Written Consent Required

Must have signed, written consent before using autodialers to call cell phones for marketing

2
Clear Opt-Out Mechanism

Every call must include instructions on how to stop future calls (usually "Reply STOP")

3
Caller ID Requirements

Must display accurate caller identification and callback number

4
Time Restrictions

Calls only allowed 8 AM to 9 PM recipient's local time

5
Agent Availability

Live agent must be available within 2 seconds of recipient answering

Text Message Compliance

SMS marketing has the strictest consent requirements:

TCPA Text Message Requirements

Express Written Consent

Must have clear, unambiguous written consent that:

  • Authorizes specific company to send texts
  • Includes your phone number
  • Discloses that consent isn't required for purchase
  • Explains standard messaging rates apply

Opt-Out Requirements

Every message must include:

  • "Reply STOP to opt out"
  • Immediate processing of STOP requests
  • Confirmation message when opted out
  • No charges for opt-out messages

High-Risk Text Scenarios

  • Purchased Lists: Never send texts to purchased contact lists
  • Business Cards: Getting a business card doesn't equal text consent
  • Lead Forms: Must explicitly ask for text permission
  • Existing Clients: Still need express consent for marketing texts

Safe Harbor Practices

Additional protective measures that go beyond minimum compliance:

Double Opt-In for Texts

Send confirmation message requiring reply before adding to text campaigns. Creates stronger consent record.

Manual Dialing for New Leads

Use manual dialing for first contact with new leads, only using autodialers after establishing relationship.

Consent Re-confirmation

Annually re-confirm text and call consent with active contacts to maintain clear permission.

Extended Documentation

Keep consent records for 7 years instead of required 5 years for added legal protection.

Industry Best Practice: The 3-Touch Rule

Limit unsolicited outreach to 3 attempts maximum:

  • Touch 1: Initial call/text with clear value proposition
  • Touch 2: Follow-up with different angle after 7 days
  • Touch 3: Final attempt after 14 days, then permanent removal

Personal Liability & Responsibilities

Individual agents and brokers can be held personally liable for violations:

Individual Liability

1
Personal Responsibility

Each person making calls or sending texts can be individually sued and fined, regardless of company policies

2
Knowledge Requirements

Ignorance of the law is not a defense - you're responsible for knowing and following all regulations

3
Due Diligence Standard

Must take reasonable steps to ensure compliance, including verifying consent and checking DNC status

4
Ongoing Obligations

Must stay current with changing regulations and maintain compliance systems throughout your career

5
Vicarious Liability

Brokers can be liable for their agents' violations, and agents for their assistants' actions
