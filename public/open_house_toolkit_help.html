Complete Workflow: From Setup to Follow-Up

Pre-Event Preparation (Day Before)

  1. Create your event in the Event Setup section with all property details
  2. Set your open house date, time, and expected visitor count
  3. Write compelling property highlights focusing on key selling points
  4. Complete the pre-event checklist to ensure you're fully prepared
  5. Test the digital sign-in form on your tablet or device
  6. Prepare any physical marketing materials and property information sheets

Event Day Setup (Day Of)

  1. Arrive early to prepare the property and stage any final touches
  2. Set up directional signage and place open house signs
  3. Position your tablet or device with the digital sign-in form prominently
  4. Ensure WiFi connectivity and device battery levels are adequate
  5. Review property highlights and prepare to discuss key features
  6. Mark remaining checklist items as complete in the system

During the Open House

  1. Greet each visitor warmly and direct them to the digital sign-in form
  2. Assist visitors with the form if needed, emphasizing the value of staying connected
  3. Engage visitors in conversation about their needs and timeline
  4. Take mental notes about each visitor's level of interest and specific questions
  5. Provide property information and answer questions about neighborhood and features
  6. Thank visitors and mention you'll follow up with additional information

Post-Event Follow-Up (Same Day/Next Day)

  1. Review all visitor entries in the Lead Capture section
  2. Set interest levels for each visitor based on your interactions
  3. Export visitor data to your main CRM or contact management system
  4. Schedule immediate follow-up calls or emails for "Very Interested" prospects
  5. Plan nurture sequences for "Somewhat Interested" visitors
  6. Send thank you emails to all visitors with additional property information

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Event Setup Problems

  • Can't Save Event Details: Ensure all required fields (marked with *) are completed before clicking "Save Event Setup"
  • Date Picker Not Working: Try clicking directly on the calendar icon or refresh the page if the date picker won't open
  • Time Fields Showing Incorrectly: Verify your browser's time zone settings and use 12-hour format with AM/PM
  • Property Type Dropdown Issues: If dropdown won't open, try clicking on the arrow or refresh the Event Setup section
  • Checklist Items Won't Check: Click directly on the checkbox or text. If issues persist, refresh the page and try again

Digital Sign-In Form Issues

  • Form Won't Load on Device: Check internet connection and refresh the Digital Sign-In tab. Ensure device has stable WiFi
  • Required Field Validation Errors: Make sure First Name, Last Name, and Email Address are all completed
  • Email Format Not Accepted: Ensure email includes @ symbol and valid domain (e.g., user@example.com)
  • Dropdown Menus Not Working: Try tapping/clicking directly on the dropdown arrow. Clear browser cache if problems persist
  • Form Submission Fails: Verify all required fields are completed and internet connection is stable before clicking "Sign In to Open House"
  • Duplicate Entries: If someone signs in twice, the system should show both entries. You can merge duplicate contacts during follow-up

Lead Capture and Management Issues

  • Visitors Not Appearing in Lead Capture: Refresh the Lead Capture section or check that form submissions completed successfully
  • Interest Level Dropdown Won't Work: Click directly on the dropdown arrow. If unresponsive, refresh the page
  • Export CSV Not Working: Ensure you have visitors in your system and try using a different browser if download fails
  • Visitor Statistics Showing Zero: Check that visitors have successfully submitted the digital sign-in form
  • Contact Information Displaying Incorrectly: Verify that visitors entered information correctly in the sign-in form

Device and Technical Issues

  • Tablet/Device Battery Drain: Keep devices plugged in during events and test battery life beforehand
  • WiFi Connection Problems: Test internet connectivity at the property before the event. Have a mobile hotspot backup
  • Screen Timeout Issues: Adjust device settings to prevent screen from timing out during the event
  • Browser Compatibility: Use updated versions of Chrome, Safari, or Edge for best compatibility
  • Touch Screen Responsiveness: Clean device screens and ensure visitors can easily tap form fields
Emergency Backup Plan: If technical issues prevent use of the digital system, keep a paper backup sign-in sheet with essential fields: Name, Email, Phone, and Interest Level. Manually enter data after the event.

Best Practices for Open House Success

Event Setup Best Practices

  • Detailed Descriptions: Write comprehensive property highlights that address common buyer concerns
  • Realistic Expectations: Set visitor expectations based on marketing reach and property appeal
  • Time Optimization: Schedule 2-3 hour windows for optimal visitor flow
  • Checklist Discipline: Always complete the full pre-event checklist to avoid missing critical steps

Digital Sign-In Optimization

  • Device Positioning: Place sign-in tablet in a prominent, well-lit location near the entrance
  • Visitor Assistance: Be available to help visitors who may be uncomfortable with digital forms
  • Value Proposition: Explain the benefits of signing in (property updates, market information, etc.)
  • Privacy Assurance: Assure visitors that their information will be used professionally

Lead Management Excellence

  • Same-Day Review: Process all visitor information within 24 hours while interactions are fresh
  • Interest Assessment: Use conversation quality and engagement level to set accurate interest ratings
  • Prompt Follow-up: Contact "Very Interested" prospects within 24-48 hours
  • Systematic Approach: Develop consistent follow-up sequences for different interest levels
Success Metric: Aim for at least 70% of visitors to complete the digital sign-in form. High completion rates indicate effective positioning and visitor engagement.

Legal and Privacy Considerations

Data Collection Compliance

  • Consent: Ensure visitors understand how their information will be used
  • Opt-in Marketing: Use the checkbox for marketing consent to comply with email regulations
  • Data Security: Protect visitor information and only share with authorized team members
  • Retention Policies: Follow appropriate data retention guidelines for your area

Professional Standards

  • Truth in Marketing: Ensure all property information is accurate and up-to-date
  • Fair Housing: Treat all visitors equally regardless of demographics
  • Professional Communication: Use visitor information only for legitimate real estate purposes
  • Privacy Protection: Don't share visitor lists with other agents or outside parties without consent
Compliance Tip: Include a brief privacy statement near your sign-in form explaining how visitor information will be used and protected.

Measuring Open House Success

Key Performance Indicators

  • Sign-in Rate: Percentage of visitors who complete the digital form
  • Lead Quality: Ratio of "Very Interested" to total visitors
  • Source Effectiveness: Which marketing channels drive the most qualified prospects
  • Follow-up Conversion: How many visitors become active clients
  • Timeline Accuracy: Whether visitor timelines match actual purchase behavior

Optimization Strategies

  • A/B Testing: Try different property descriptions and see which generate more interest
  • Time Analysis: Track which time slots attract the highest-quality visitors
  • Marketing Attribution: Double down on channels that bring serious buyers
  • Seasonal Patterns: Identify optimal times of year for your market

ROI Calculation

Calculate your open house return on investment by tracking:

  • Marketing costs (advertising, signage, materials)
  • Time investment (preparation, event, follow-up)
  • Leads generated and their quality scores
  • Conversion rates from leads to appointments
  • Final commissions earned from open house leads
Success Benchmark: Aim for at least 15-20% of your visitors to show "Very Interested" or "Somewhat Interested" levels for a successful event.

Seasonal and Market Adaptations

Peak Season Optimization (Spring/Summer)

  • Higher Expectations: Increase expected visitor counts during peak buying seasons
  • Competitive Positioning: Use more detailed property highlights to stand out
  • Extended Hours: Consider longer open house windows during busy periods
  • Multiple Sessions: Run back-to-back events to accommodate higher demand

Off-Season Strategies (Fall/Winter)

  • Quality Focus: Fewer visitors but potentially more serious buyers
  • Virtual Integration: Combine in-person events with virtual tour options
  • Incentive Messaging: Highlight advantages of off-season buying
  • Cozy Presentation: Emphasize comfort features during colder months

Market Condition Adjustments

  • Seller's Market: Focus on qualifying serious buyers and managing crowds
  • Buyer's Market: Emphasize unique property features and competitive advantages
  • Price-Sensitive Markets: Highlight value propositions and cost-saving features
  • Luxury Markets: Ensure high-end presentation and detailed amenity descriptions
