Section 1: What is Skip Tracing?

Estimated Reading Time: 12 minutes

Skip tracing is the investigative process of locating people who have "skipped" or moved away from their known addresses without leaving forwarding information. In real estate investing, skip tracing is used to find current contact information for property owners when public records don't have accurate phone numbers or current addresses.

üí° Key Definition

Skip Tracing: The process of using various databases, public records, and investigative techniques to locate current contact information for individuals who are difficult to reach through conventional means.

The Origin of "Skip Tracing"

The term "skip tracing" comes from the word "skip" - meaning someone who has left or "skipped town" without paying debts or maintaining contact. Originally used by debt collectors and private investigators, skip tracing has become an essential tool for real estate investors seeking to contact property owners directly.

How Skip Tracing Works

Professional skip tracers use a combination of:

  • Database Searches: Access to proprietary databases containing phone numbers, addresses, and personal information
  • Public Records: Court records, voter registrations, property transfers, and business filings
  • Social Media Investigation: Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, and other platforms
  • Utility Records: Electric, gas, water, and cable company records
  • Employment Records: Current and previous employment information
  • Relative Searches: Finding family members who might have current contact information

üì± Real-World Example

Scenario: You find a vacant house with an owner listed as "John Smith" in the county records. The address on file is from 2015, and the phone number is disconnected.

Skip Tracing Process:

  • Search databases for John Smith's current address
  • Find his current phone number and email
  • Locate relatives who might know how to reach him
  • Discover he moved to Florida and now has a new number

Result: You can now contact John directly about his vacant property instead of sending blind mail to an old address.

What Makes Skip Tracing Different from Google Searches

While anyone can search Google or social media, professional skip tracing goes much deeper:

  • Access to Private Databases: Information not available to the general public
  • Cross-Reference Verification: Multiple sources confirm accuracy
  • Historical Data: Past addresses and phone numbers to track movement patterns
  • Advanced Search Techniques: Professional tools and methodologies
  • Legal Compliance: Following proper procedures and regulations
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Skip tracing must be conducted legally and ethically, following all federal and state regulations regarding privacy and data use.

Section 2: Why Skip Tracing is Essential for Real Estate

Estimated Reading Time: 15 minutes

In real estate investing, your success depends on your ability to find and contact motivated property owners. Skip tracing gives you a significant competitive advantage by helping you reach owners that other investors can't find.

The Problem with Public Records

Public records are often outdated or incomplete because:

  • No Update Requirements: Property owners aren't required to update contact information
  • Privacy Concerns: Many people use P.O. boxes or old addresses for privacy
  • Life Changes: People move, change phone numbers, get divorced, or pass away
  • Out-of-State Owners: Investment property owners may live far from their properties
  • Business Ownership: Properties owned by LLCs or trusts hide individual contact information

üéØ With Skip Tracing

  • Current, verified phone numbers
  • Multiple contact methods (phone, email, address)
  • Higher response rates
  • Reach motivated sellers first
  • Bypass competition
  • Connect with out-of-state owners

üìã Public Records Only

  • Often outdated phone numbers
  • Limited to mailing addresses
  • Low response rates
  • Same leads as every other investor
  • High competition
  • Miss many opportunities

Specific Real Estate Scenarios Where Skip Tracing Excels

1. Vacant Properties

Vacant homes often have the most outdated contact information. Property owners may have:

  • Moved out of state
  • Inherited the property
  • Abandoned the property due to financial hardship
  • Moved to assisted living facilities

2. Distressed Properties

Properties in foreclosure, tax lien, or code violation situations often have owners who:

  • Are avoiding creditors
  • Have disconnected phones
  • Moved to avoid the situation
  • Are highly motivated to sell

3. Inherited Properties

When properties are inherited, the new owners often:

  • Live far from the property
  • Don't want to maintain the property
  • Need quick cash for estate settlement
  • Haven't updated public records yet

Section 3: Understanding Skip Trace Data Quality

Estimated Reading Time: 18 minutes

Not all skip trace data is created equal. Understanding how to evaluate data quality is crucial for maximizing your success rate and avoiding wasted time on bad leads.

Confidence Scores Explained

Professional skip tracing services provide confidence scores that indicate how reliable the data is. These scores are typically based on:

üéØ Confidence Score Factors

  • Source Verification: How many databases confirmed the information
  • Recency: How recently the information was updated
  • Cross-Reference: Whether multiple sources show the same data
  • Activity: Recent activity on phone numbers or addresses
  • Match Quality: How closely the data matches the search criteria

Confidence Score Ranges

High Confidence (80-100%)

  • Multiple database confirmations
  • Recent activity verification
  • Cross-referenced sources
  • Priority for calling
  • Expected success rate: 10-25%

Very Low Confidence (0-29%)

  • Unverified information
  • Very old data
  • Single source only
  • Often skip these leads
  • Expected success rate: 5-15%

Key Data Fields to Evaluate

Phone Numbers

Phone numbers are usually your primary contact method. Look for:

  • Line Type: Mobile vs landline (mobile often better)
  • Carrier Information: Active carriers vs VOIP services
  • Port Date: When the number was last moved between carriers
  • Multiple Numbers: Having backup numbers increases success
  • Verification Status: Recently verified vs old data

Email Addresses

Email provides another contact channel and insights:

  • Domain Type: Gmail, Yahoo, or professional domains
  • Verification: Whether the email address is active
  • Associated Accounts: Linked to social media or other services
  • Creation Date: Newer emails may be more accurate

Addresses

Current address helps with additional contact methods:

  • Residential vs Commercial: Type of address found
  • Move-in Date: How recently they moved to this address
  • Address Verification: Confirmed through multiple sources
  • Property Ownership: Do they own or rent at this address

Red Flags to Watch For

üö® Data Quality Red Flags

Phone Number Red Flags

  • Disconnected Numbers: No longer in service
  • Google Voice/VOIP: May indicate person is hiding
  • Business Numbers: Not personal contact information
  • Outdated Area Codes: Area code doesn't match current location
  • Sequential Numbers: Fake or placeholder numbers

Email Red Flags

  • Bounced Emails: Address no longer active
  • Temporary Email Services: 10-minute mail type services
  • Obvious Fakes: test@test.com or similar patterns
  • Very Old Domains: AOL, Hotmail from many years ago

Address Red Flags

  • P.O. Boxes Only: No residential address found
  • Commercial Addresses: Business locations instead of homes
  • Incomplete Addresses: Missing apartment numbers or details
  • Multiple Recent Moves: Frequent address changes

Section 4: Working with Skip Trace Providers

Estimated Reading Time: 15 minutes

Since your CRM platform partners with professional skip tracing companies, understanding how to work effectively with these providers will maximize your results and minimize costs.

Types of Skip Trace Providers

1. Bulk Skip Tracing Services

Best for processing large lists of properties:

  • Volume Discounts: Lower per-record costs for larger orders
  • Batch Processing: Upload entire lists for processing
  • Turnaround Time: Usually 24-72 hours for results
  • Cost Range: $0.50-$3.00 per record
  • Best For: Monthly campaigns, large property lists

2. Real-Time Skip Tracing

Instant results for individual properties:

  • Immediate Results: Get data within seconds or minutes
  • Higher Cost: Premium pricing for instant service
  • API Integration: Works seamlessly with CRM systems
  • Cost Range: $2.00-$10.00 per record
  • Best For: Hot leads, time-sensitive opportunities

3. Premium Deep Skip Tracing

Manual research for difficult cases:

  • Human Research: Professional investigators handle complex cases
  • Comprehensive Reports: Detailed background information
  • Higher Success Rate: Better results for challenging searches
  • Cost Range: $25-$100+ per record
  • Best For: High-value properties, complex ownership
