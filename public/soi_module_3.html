Section 1: The 33-Touch System

Estimated Reading Time: 15 minutes

The 33-Touch System is a proven framework that ensures you maintain meaningful contact with your SOI throughout the year. The magic number 33 represents the optimal frequency to stay top-of-mind without being overwhelming or annoying.

Why 33 Touches Work

Research in consumer psychology shows that it takes 7-12 exposures for people to remember a brand, but 20+ exposures to consider taking action. The 33-touch system ensures you're always in the "consideration zone" when real estate needs arise.

The Science Behind Touch Point Frequency

Marketing studies reveal optimal contact frequencies for relationship maintenance:

Optimal Frequency Range

  • 24-36 touches annually: Stay top-of-mind
  • Under 20 touches: Risk being forgotten
  • Over 50 touches: Risk being annoying
  • 33 touches: Sweet spot for engagement

Touch Point Distribution

  • Email: 18 touches (55%)
  • Personal calls: 6 touches (18%)
  • Social media: 5 touches (15%)
  • Events/gatherings: 2 touches (6%)
  • Direct mail: 2 touches (6%)

Section 2: Multi-Channel Communication Strategy

Estimated Reading Time: 12 minutes

Modern SOI management requires a multi-channel approach. Different people prefer different communication methods, and using multiple channels increases your chances of meaningful engagement while reinforcing your message.

The Communication Channel Matrix

Understanding when and how to use each communication channel optimizes your touch point effectiveness:

High-Touch Channels

  • Phone Calls: Immediate, personal, high impact
  • Face-to-Face: Strongest relationship building
  • Personal Notes: Thoughtful, memorable
  • Video Messages: Personal scale, efficient

Best for: Tier 1 VIPs, life events, referral follow-up

Scalable Channels

  • Email: Detailed content, trackable
  • Text Messages: Immediate, high open rates
  • Social Media: Public engagement, viral potential
  • Direct Mail: Physical presence, novelty factor

Best for: Tier 2-3 contacts, market updates, events

Channel-Specific Strategies

Email Communication Excellence

Email remains the backbone of SOI communication when done properly:

High-Performing Email Framework

  • Subject Line: Specific, local, valuable (not salesy)
  • Opening: Personal connection or shared experience
  • Value Content: Market insight, helpful tip, or resource
  • Soft Call-to-Action: "Let me know if you have questions"
  • Personal Signature: Photo, direct phone, social links

Example Subject Lines That Work:

  • "Oak Hill home values up 12% this quarter"
  • "New coffee shop opening on Main Street"
  • "Your neighbor just sold - thought you'd be interested"
  • "Winter home maintenance checklist for our area"
