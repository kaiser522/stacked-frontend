What is the Deal Pipeline?

The Deal Pipeline is a visual Kanban board that helps you track and manage real estate deals from initial lead to closing. It provides a drag-and-drop interface where you can organize deals into customizable columns representing different stages of your sales process.

This powerful tool gives you an at-a-glance view of your entire deal flow, making it easy to see where each property stands, identify bottlenecks, and move deals forward efficiently. The visual nature of the Kanban system helps you prioritize your efforts and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

Kanban Components

  • Columns: Represent different stages in your deal process (e.g., New Leads, Contacted, Appointment, Offer Made, Contract, Closed)
  • Cards: Individual deals/properties that move through your pipeline
  • Column Count: Number of deals currently in each stage
  • Card Details: Property address, location, status tags, and deal value

Default Pipeline Stages

The Deal Pipeline comes with common real estate sales stages, but you can customize these to fit your process:

Standard Columns

  • New Leads: Recently acquired leads that haven't been contacted yet
  • Contacted: Leads you've reached out to and had initial communication
  • Appointment: Deals where you've scheduled or completed a showing or meeting
  • Offer Made: Properties where you've submitted an offer to the seller
  • Contract: Deals under contract, awaiting closing
  • Closed: Successfully completed transactions
Customization Tip: Your pipeline stages should reflect your actual sales process. If you work with investors, you might include stages like "Property Evaluation" or "Investor Pitch." For traditional residential sales, consider stages like "Pre-Approval" or "Inspection."

Adding a New Column

Create custom pipeline stages to match your unique workflow:

  1. Click the "+ Add Column" button in the top-right corner
  2. A new column placeholder appears with an input field
  3. Enter your column title (e.g., "Negotiation", "Due Diligence", "Under Inspection")
  4. Click the green "‚úì Add" button to confirm
  5. Or click "‚úó Cancel" to discard the new column
  6. Your new column appears in the pipeline, ready for cards

Column Naming Best Practices

  • Use clear, action-oriented names that indicate the stage (e.g., "Awaiting Response" not just "Waiting")
  • Keep names concise for better visual layout (2-3 words maximum)
  • Use consistent terminology across your team
  • Order columns left-to-right from earliest to latest stage
Column Strategy: Most effective pipelines have 5-8 columns. Too few and you lose detail; too many and the board becomes cluttered. Focus on stages where deals actually pause for action or decision-making.

Managing Columns

Editing Column Titles

  1. Click the edit icon (pencil) next to the column title
  2. The column title becomes editable
  3. Type your new column name
  4. Press Enter or click outside to save
  5. The column updates with the new name

Deleting Columns

  1. Click the delete icon (trash can) next to the column title
  2. Confirm you want to delete the column
  3. The column is removed from your pipeline
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Deleting a column that contains deals will also delete all cards in that column. Make sure to move important deals to another column before deleting. This action cannot be undone.

Reordering Columns

  • Columns can be reordered by dragging them left or right
  • Click and hold the column header to start dragging
  • Move the column to your desired position
  • Release to drop the column in its new location
  • Your pipeline order is saved automatically

Adding Deal Cards

Add new deals to your pipeline quickly and easily:

  1. Click the "+ Add Card" button at the bottom of any column
  2. A form appears for entering deal details
  3. Enter the property address as the card title
  4. Add the full location (City, State, ZIP)
  5. Select a status tag (Vacant, Rental, Payments, etc.)
  6. Enter the property value or deal amount
  7. Add any relevant notes or timeline information
  8. Click "Add" to create the card in that column

Card Information Fields

  • Property Address: Street address and property identifier
  • Location: City, state, and ZIP code for quick reference
  • Status Tag: Color-coded label indicating property status or deal type
  • Value: Property value, asking price, or expected deal amount
  • Timestamp: Automatically tracks when added or when action occurred

Status Tags and Labels

Use color-coded tags to quickly identify deal characteristics:

Common Tag Uses

  • Vacant: Empty properties that may need quick action
  • Rental: Currently rented properties with tenant considerations
  • Payments: Sellers behind on mortgage payments (motivated)
  • Tired Owner: Landlords ready to exit rental business
  • Inherited: Properties inherited by sellers who want quick sales
  • Divorce: Properties being sold due to divorce (time-sensitive)

Creating Custom Tags

Customize tags to match the deal types and situations you encounter most:

  • Probate - For estate sales
  • Pre-Foreclosure - Properties facing foreclosure
  • Investor - Deals with investment buyers
  • Fix & Flip - Renovation opportunities
  • Wholesale - Deals intended for wholesaling
Tag Strategy: Limit yourself to 6-8 primary tags that represent your most common deal types. Too many tags creates confusion and reduces their effectiveness for quick visual scanning.

Moving Deals Through the Pipeline

Drag-and-Drop Functionality

  1. Click and hold on any deal card
  2. Drag the card horizontally to another column
  3. Hover over the target column until it highlights
  4. Release the mouse to drop the card into the new column
  5. The card updates automatically with the new stage

When to Move Deals

  • New Leads ‚Üí Contacted: After making first contact with the seller
  • Contacted ‚Üí Appointment: When you've scheduled a showing or meeting
  • Appointment ‚Üí Offer Made: After submitting your offer to the seller
  • Offer Made ‚Üí Contract: When the offer is accepted and contract is signed
  • Contract ‚Üí Closed: After successful closing and deal completion
Pipeline Discipline: Move deals promptly when actions occur. A current pipeline is a useful pipeline. Make updating the board part of your daily routine‚Äîafter phone calls, after meetings, after sending offers.

Editing and Deleting Cards

Editing Deal Information

  1. Click the edit icon (pencil) on any deal card
  2. The card details open in edit mode
  3. Modify any information: address, value, tag, notes
  4. Save your changes
  5. The card updates immediately on the board

Deleting Deals

  1. Click the delete icon (trash can) on the deal card
  2. Confirm you want to delete this deal
  3. The card is permanently removed from the pipeline
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Deleted deals cannot be recovered. Before deleting, consider if you should move the deal to an "Archive" or "Dead Deals" column instead, preserving it for future reference or potential revival.

Pipeline Best Practices

Daily Pipeline Management

  • Review your pipeline every morning to plan your day
  • Move cards immediately after completing actions
  • Add new leads to the pipeline as soon as they come in
  • Update deal values when you get new information
  • Check for stale deals that haven't moved in weeks

Weekly Pipeline Review

  • Identify deals that are stuck in one stage too long
  • Calculate total value in each column to see where your money is
  • Remove or archive dead deals that won't move forward
  • Ensure all recent leads have been properly categorized
  • Plan follow-up actions for deals in each stage

Optimizing Your Pipeline

  • Balance Your Columns: Aim for deals in every stage for consistent closings
  • Address Bottlenecks: If one column is always full, that's where deals are stalling
  • Track Velocity: Notice how long deals typically stay in each stage
  • Use Tags Consistently: Standardize tag usage across your team
  • Keep It Current: An outdated pipeline is worse than no pipeline
Pipeline Metrics: Count how many deals are in each column and track the total value. If you have $500K in contracts and typically close 50%, you can project $250K in upcoming closings. This helps with financial planning.

Troubleshooting

Cards Not Dragging

  • Ensure you're clicking and holding on the card itself, not the icons
  • Try refreshing your browser if drag-and-drop becomes unresponsive
  • Check that you have a stable internet connection
  • Clear browser cache if issues persist

Changes Not Saving

  • Verify you have an active internet connection
  • Look for save confirmation messages or indicators
  • Refresh the page to see if changes were actually saved
  • Avoid making changes in multiple browser tabs simultaneously

Column or Card Disappeared

  • Check if it was accidentally moved to another column
  • Verify you didn't accidentally delete it
  • Refresh the page to reload all data
  • Contact support if data loss persists

Pipeline Layout Issues

  • Try zooming out if columns are cut off (Ctrl/Cmd + minus key)
  • Scroll horizontally to see additional columns
  • Consider using fewer columns if the board becomes too wide
  • Use full-screen mode for better visibility
