What is the Client Engagement Scorecard?

The Client Engagement Scorecard is an advanced analytics tool that automatically tracks and analyzes your email communication patterns with clients. It provides real-time insights into how effectively you're engaging with your client base by monitoring email opens, reply rates, response times, and overall engagement levels.

This powerful feature helps you identify which clients need more attention, track your communication effectiveness over time, and make data-driven decisions about where to focus your follow-up efforts. The scorecard integrates with your email platform to provide automatic, real-time updates without any manual data entry.

Engagement Score Calculation

The engagement score is calculated using multiple factors:

  • Email Open Rate: Percentage of your emails the client opens (40% weight)
  • Reply Rate: How often the client responds to your emails (30% weight)
  • Response Speed: How quickly they reply when they do respond (20% weight)
  • Recent Activity: More recent interactions are weighted higher (10% weight)

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does the scorecard update?

The scorecard updates in real-time as email events occur. When a client opens an email or sends a reply, their engagement score recalculates automatically within minutes.

Why is my response time different from what I expected?

Response time is calculated from when a client sends you an email to when you reply. It's not about how long it takes you to send emails. Make sure you're measuring your reply speed, not your outbound email timing.

Can clients see their engagement scores?

No. Engagement scores are internal analytics tools visible only to you and authorized team members. Clients never see their scores or know they're being tracked.

What if a client prefers phone communication?

Clients who prefer phone calls will naturally have lower email engagement scores. This doesn't mean they're not engaged‚Äîit means email isn't their preferred channel. Use the scorecard to identify this pattern, then adjust your communication method.

How many emails do I need before scores are accurate?

Engagement scores become reliable after sending at least 5-10 emails to a client. Very new clients may have incomplete or fluctuating scores until more data is collected.

Why did a client's score suddenly drop?

Sudden score drops can indicate: they're on vacation, they changed email addresses, emails are going to spam, they lost interest in buying/selling, or they're working with another agent. Investigate and reach out through a different channel.

Should I remove low engagement clients?

Not immediately. First, try re-engagement strategies and alternative communication methods. If after multiple attempts they remain unresponsive for 90+ days, they may need to be moved to a "cold leads" category rather than deleted.

Can I manually adjust engagement scores?

No. Scores are automatically calculated based on actual email behavior to ensure objectivity and accuracy. You cannot manually change scores.

Does the scorecard track other communication channels?

Currently, the scorecard only tracks email communication. Phone calls, texts, and in-person meetings are not included in engagement scoring. Future updates may include additional channels.
