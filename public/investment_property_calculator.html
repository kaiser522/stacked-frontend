Understanding Investment Metrics

Learn what each metric means and how to use them to evaluate investment properties.

Cash Flow

Monthly rental income minus all expenses including mortgage payment. Positive cash flow means the property pays for itself plus extra income.

Cash-on-Cash Return

Annual cash flow divided by total cash invested. Shows the return on your actual cash investment. Target: 8-12%+

Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)

Net operating income divided by property value. Measures property's earning potential independent of financing. Higher = better return.

1% Rule

Monthly rent should equal at least 1% of purchase price. Quick screening tool - properties meeting this rule often cash flow positive.

ROI (Return on Investment)

Total annual return including cash flow, appreciation, tax benefits, and principal paydown divided by cash invested.

BRRRR Strategy

Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat. Strategy to recycle capital by refinancing after adding value through renovations.

Investment Strategy Guidelines

Key considerations when evaluating and presenting investment opportunities to clients.

What Makes a Good Investment Property

Location: Growing neighborhoods with job growth and amenities
Rent-to-Price Ratio: Meets or exceeds 1% rule for cash flow
Condition: Move-in ready or value-add opportunity with clear rehab plan
Market Fundamentals: Strong rental demand and limited supply

Client Consultation Points

Red Flags to Avoid

Negative Cash Flow: Properties requiring monthly contributions
Declining Neighborhoods: Areas with job losses or declining values
High Maintenance Properties: Old buildings needing constant repairs
Oversaturated Markets: Too many rentals competing for tenants
