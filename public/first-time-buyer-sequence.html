The Inspection Process
Day 22
Subject: Your home inspection guide (what to expect)

Hi [First Name],

Once your offer is accepted (exciting!), the next big step is the home inspection. This is where we find out what's really going on with the house.

What happens during an inspection?
A professional inspector will spend 2-4 hours checking everything from the roof to the foundation. They'll look at:

‚Ä¢ Roof, attic, and exterior
‚Ä¢ Foundation and structure
‚Ä¢ Plumbing and electrical
‚Ä¢ HVAC systems
‚Ä¢ Appliances
‚Ä¢ Windows and doors
‚Ä¢ Basically everything!

Should you attend the inspection?

YES! I always recommend being there for at least the last hour. The inspector will walk you through their findings, and you can ask questions. Plus, you'll learn a ton about your new home.

After the inspection:
You'll get a detailed report (usually 30-50 pages). Don't freak out if there are issues - almost every home has something. The question is: what's a big deal and what isn't?

Your options if issues are found:
1. Ask the seller to fix it
2. Ask for a credit at closing
3. Negotiate a lower price
4. Walk away if it's really bad
5. Accept it and move forward

I'll help you decide what's worth negotiating and what's not. Remember - you're looking for major issues (roof, foundation, electrical, plumbing), not minor cosmetic stuff.

I work with great inspectors who are thorough but fair. When you're ready, I'll get you connected!
