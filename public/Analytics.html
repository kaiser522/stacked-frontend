What is the Analytics Section?

The Analytics section is your comprehensive business intelligence dashboard that provides deep insights into your real estate performance. It combines contact source analysis, pipeline analytics, activity tracking with smart prioritization, and performance metrics to help you make data-driven decisions and optimize your business strategy.

This advanced section uses intelligent algorithms to calculate client priority scores, track deal flow through your pipeline, monitor activity completion rates, and analyze which marketing channels deliver the best ROI. Everything updates in real-time to give you an accurate picture of your business health.

Priority Scoring System

The Activity Hub uses an intelligent point-based system to automatically prioritize your clients. Points accumulate based on various factors, and clients are categorized into three priority levels:

Priority Levels

High Priority (80-100 points)

Clients requiring immediate attention. These are hot leads, time-sensitive situations, or clients you haven't contacted in too long.

Medium Priority (40-79 points)

Clients needing follow-up soon but not urgent. Regular check-ins and scheduled activities.

Low Priority (10-39 points)

Routine follow-ups and relationship maintenance. Birthdays, newsletters, general check-ins.

High Priority Triggers (80-100 points)

  • No contact 10+ days: +30 points - Client hasn't been contacted in over 10 days
  • Hot lead (viewed 3+ properties): +25 points - Indicates serious buying intent
  • Pre-approval expiring (‚â§7 days): +35 points - Time-sensitive financing window
  • Under contract with deadline: +40 points - Critical milestone approaching
  • Missed appointment follow-up: +30 points - Need to reconnect after missed meeting

Medium Priority Triggers (40-79 points)

  • No contact 5-9 days: +15 points - Approaching too long without contact
  • Showing follow-up needed: +20 points - Need feedback from recent showing
  • Document pending: +15 points - Waiting on client paperwork
  • Market update requested: +10 points - Client asked for information
  • Scheduling needed: +15 points - Appointment needs to be set

Low Priority Triggers (10-39 points)

  • Regular check-in: +5 points - Routine relationship maintenance
  • Newsletter/marketing: +5 points - Content distribution opportunity
  • Birthday/anniversary: +10 points - Personal touch point
  • General follow-up: +5 points - Non-urgent communication
  • Referral opportunity: +12 points - Potential for new business
üí° How Priority Works: Points accumulate across multiple factors. A client with no contact for 10 days (+30) who viewed 3+ properties (+25) would score 55 points (Medium Priority). The system updates automatically as situations change.

Maximizing Client Priority System

Best Practices for Priority Management

  • Act on High Priority Immediately: Clients scoring 80+ points need same-day attention
  • Don't Let Medium Become High: Address 40-79 point clients within 48 hours
  • Schedule Low Priority Systematically: Set recurring weekly time for low-priority relationship maintenance
  • Log Every Interaction: The system works best when you consistently record activities
  • Review Point Thresholds: Adjust in settings if you find certain triggers too aggressive or too lenient

Common Priority Scenarios

  • Hot Lead + No Contact = Urgent: High-interest client you haven't touched base with needs immediate outreach
  • Pre-approval Expiring: Time-sensitive financing situation requires quick action to keep deal alive
  • Under Contract + Deadline: Critical milestone approaching, ensure everything is on track
  • Missed Appointment: Re-engage quickly to maintain relationship and reschedule
  • Birthday/Anniversary: Low-effort, high-impact touch point for relationship building
Priority Strategy: Aim to keep your high-priority list under 5 clients. If you consistently have more than 5 high-priority clients, you're either not following up quickly enough or your point thresholds need adjustment in settings.

Understanding Pipeline Value

How Pipeline Value Works

The Pipeline Value section breaks down the total monetary value of your deals:

  • Total Pipeline Value: Sum of all active deals regardless of stage
  • Offer Stage Value: Total value where you've made offers but not yet under contract
  • Contract Stage Value: Total value of deals under contract (most likely to close)
  • Active Pipeline Value: All opportunities currently being worked

Using Pipeline Value for Forecasting

Apply historical conversion rates to pipeline value for revenue projections:

  • If your conversion rate is 27% and total pipeline is $848,500, projected closings ‚âà $229K
  • Contract stage has highest conversion (typically 60-80%), so $128K in contracts ‚âà $77-102K in expected revenue
  • Track how pipeline value changes month-over-month as a leading indicator
  • Declining pipeline value means you need more lead generation

Healthy Pipeline Indicators

  • Pipeline value should be 3-5x your monthly revenue goal
  • Contract stage should be 40-60% of total pipeline value
  • Offer stage should represent 20-30% of pipeline
  • If pipeline value is declining for 2+ months, increase lead generation

  • Too Many in Contract: Great! But ensure new leads are coming in for next month
  • Empty Early Stages: Lead generation problem - need more top-of-funnel activity
  • Empty Late Stages: Conversion problem - leads not progressing to close

  • Lower rates suggest lead quality issues or inadequate nurturing

Optimizing Contact Source Performance

Calculating ROI by Source

Use the source performance data to calculate return on investment:

  • Formula: (Revenue - Cost) / Cost √ó 100 = ROI%
  • Example: Referrals generated $145K with $5K cost = ($145K - $5K) / $5K = 2,800% ROI
  • Track costs for each source (advertising, event costs, etc.)
  • Compare ROI across sources to guide budget allocation

When to Double Down on a Source

Increase investment in a source when it shows:

  • Conversion rate above 25%
  • ROI exceeding 500%
  • Consistent performance over 3+ months
  • Room for scaling (can generate more volume)

When to Cut a Source

Reduce or eliminate sources that show:

  • Conversion rate below 10% for 6+ months
  • Negative or very low ROI (under 200%)
  • High time investment with minimal results
  • Declining performance trend over time

Testing New Sources

  • Allocate 10-20% of marketing budget to testing
  • Give new sources at least 3 months and 20+ leads to evaluate
  • Track all costs carefully to calculate accurate ROI
  • Compare new source performance to established sources

Troubleshooting Analytics

Priority Scores Seem Wrong

  • Verify you're logging all activities - missing data skews scores
  • Check Settings to review point values for each trigger
  • Ensure client information is up-to-date (last contact date, etc.)
  • Some triggers may need adjustment based on your business model
  • Contact support if scores still seem inaccurate after checking these items

Conversion Rate Not Calculating

  • Ensure deals are properly marked as closed in the system
  • Verify lead dates are accurately recorded
  • Check that you have sufficient data (need at least 10 deals)
  • Refresh the page to force recalculation
  • Make sure lead source is assigned to all contacts

Pipeline Value Doesn't Match Expectations

  • Confirm all deal values are entered correctly
  • Check that closed deals are moved to proper stage
  • Verify deals aren't duplicated across stages
  • Ensure old/dead deals are archived or deleted
  • Review stage definitions - deals may be miscategorized

Activity Feed Not Showing Recent Activities

  • Refresh the page to load latest data
  • Check Timeline vs List view toggle
  • Verify activities were saved properly
  • Look for date filters that might be hiding activities
  • Clear browser cache if issues persist

Advanced Analytics Strategies

Predictive Revenue Forecasting

  1. Take your contract stage value ($128K in example)
  2. Apply historical close rate (if 70% of contracts close = $89.6K)
  3. Take offer stage value ($165K) and apply offer-to-contract rate (typically 40% = $66K)
  4. Apply contract close rate to that ($66K √ó 70% = $46.2K)
  5. Total forecasted revenue = $89.6K + $46.2K = $135.8K
  6. Add time-to-close to project when revenue will hit
